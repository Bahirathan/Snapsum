## 🎯 2. Keyword & Intent Opportunity Map

Passive search terms are highly saturated and costly to target. We must capture users exhibiting **high transactional intent** (searching for active study solutions) and **pain-point indicators** (complaining about existing study limits).

### High-Yield Search Categories & Keyword Mapping:

1. **Category A: Active Lecture Conversion (Primary Target)**
   * *Target Keywords:* `convert youtube lecture to quiz`, `make flashcards from video lecture`, `ai lecture to study guide`.
   * *User Intent:* Students with an imminent exam seeking immediate, hands-on self-testing tools.
   * *Search Volume / Difficulty:* Medium volume / Low difficulty (unexplored by major players who target generic "summarize" queries).

2. **Category B: Structured Study Methodologies**
   * *Target Keywords:* `online cornell notes generator`, `feynman technique learning bot`, `active recall study planner free`.
   * *User Intent:* Serious self-learners, medical/legal students seeking systematic, evidence-based studying.
   * *Search Volume / Difficulty:* High volume / Medium difficulty.

3. **Category C: Alternative-Seekers (Poaching Competitors)**
   * *Target Keywords:* `free notebooklm alternatives`, `merlin extension alternative`, `import pdf to flashcards free`.
   * *User Intent:* Budget-conscious, friction-fatigued users looking to switch platforms.
   * *Search Volume / Difficulty:* Low-Medium volume / Extremely low difficulty (high conversion).

## 🤖 3. The GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) Strategy

Modern learners do not just search Google; they query AI search engines (Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT Search, Gemini). To win recommendations inside these platforms, Zipytiny implements a strict GEO protocol.

### How LLM Search Agents Read & Recommend Zipytiny:
1. **Semantic HTML Structure:** We compile a hidden, highly structured semantic HTML block (such as our programmatic `/tools/*` landing pages) containing clean target headings and contextual paragraphs designed for LLM scrapers.
2. **Schema markup (JSON-LD):** Every page injects a schema structure defining our active recall modules, and our FAQ page embeds a schema-compliant `FAQPage` structure.
3. **Structured Microdata Answer Mapping:** By serving direct question-answer pairings (e.g. *"What is an active recall study generator?"*), we fit perfectly into Google AI Overviews' "Featured Snippet" selectors.
4. **Brand Trust Association:** By hosting a crawlable, lightweight `/llms.txt` file and linking it in our robots.txt, we present a standardized API-like summary of Zipytiny's capabilities to crawling AI systems.

## 🧬 5. The Spaced Repetition Loop (The Retention Engine)

To move from a single-visit utility to a daily recurring learning habit, Zipytiny must establish a strong **Product Hook**:

```
      [ 1. Trigger ] ──────► High-friction educational input (dense PDF/long lecture)
            ▲
            │ (Day 30 Review)
            │
      [ 4. Investment ] ───► Spaced repetition study schedule (SM-2 Algorithm)
            ▲
            │ (Day 7 Socratic)
            │
      [ 3. Reward ] ───────► Perfect comprehension, immediate progress charts
            ▲
            │ (Day 3 Quiz)
            │
      [ 2. Action ] ───────► Instant summary & auto-generated Cornell notes (Day 1)
```

1. **Day 1 (Acquisition & Structuring):** User imports source material. Zipytiny segments the transcript, writes Milestones, and builds an active-recall study guide.
2. **Day 3 (The Diagnostic Test):** The platform prompts the user with an interactive quiz, tracking weak conceptual areas.
3. **Day 7 (Socratic Intervention):** Our Socratic Tutor engages the user to talk through their weak areas using simple explanations (the Feynman technique).
4. **Day 30 (Reinforcement):** Based on the SM-2 spaced repetition scheduler, the user is prompted to do a quick review deck to lock the memories in permanently.
